Distributed BlockChain
Another way the blockchains can secure themselves is by being distributed. Instead of a central authority to manage the chain, blockchains use a peer to peer networks that anyone can join. When someone joins the network, they get a full copy of the blockchain. Whenever a new block is added, a copy of it is send to each node in the network. After the verification of the block by each node, the block is appended to the existing chain. Thus, consensus amongst all the nodes, has to be reached for addition of a new block.
